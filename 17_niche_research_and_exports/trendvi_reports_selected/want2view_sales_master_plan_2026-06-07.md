# WANT2VIEW Sales Master Plan

Date: 2026-06-07

## Short Answer: 50 Leads Is Not Enough

50 leads is enough for a first signal test, not enough for a sales system.

Use the current 50 as Wave 1:
- validate positioning;
- see which segment replies;
- learn which offer gets niche submissions;
- create the first 3-5 demo briefs.

Then scale to:
- 150 leads for learning;
- 300-500 leads for repeatable outbound;
- 1,000+ only after one segment and one offer clearly works.

The goal is not volume first. The goal is a repeatable loop:

> Find relevant agency -> identify their research vulnerability -> offer a free mini scan -> deliver useful demo -> convert to full research/workspace.

## Product Sales Thesis

We are not selling "analytics software".

We are selling:

> A faster way for agencies and content teams to create evidence-backed content, campaign, and production briefs.

The buyer does not wake up wanting another dashboard. They wake up needing:
- stronger client strategy;
- faster research;
- better content topics;
- campaign angles;
- proof before production;
- less manual competitor digging;
- a way to look smarter in front of clients.

## Primary Sales Motion

### Motion 1: Free Mini Research

This is the main wedge.

CTA:
> Send one niche, channel, or client category. I will run a free mini scan and send back a short research brief.

Why it works:
- Low friction.
- No meeting required.
- Shows value before asking for payment.
- Lets the lead give us context.
- Creates a natural reason to follow up.

### Motion 2: Prepared Demo Workspace

Use only after interest.

CTA:
> I prepared a demo workspace for this niche so your team can click through the channels, videos, patterns, and brief structure.

Why not lead with this:
- A blank account is weak.
- People do not explore dashboards cold.
- The brief creates desire; the account supports trust.

### Motion 3: Paid Pilot

CTA:
> Let us test one full research report or one active workspace for one client. If it saves your team time, expand to agency mode.

## Funnel Math

- 300 targeted leads;
- 60-75% deliverability/reach;
- 5-10% positive/curious replies;
- 40-60% of replies give a niche or accept mini scan;
- 10-20% of delivered demos turn into a paid test;

Expected from 300 strong leads:
- 15-30 positive replies;

- 1-4 paid tests;

This is why 50 is not enough. With 50, we mainly learn message-market fit.

## Channel Strategy

### Channel A: Cold Email

Purpose:
- primary scalable channel.

Use for:
- agencies with public email;
- founder-led websites;
- contact forms where email is absent.

Rules:
- send from a dedicated outreach domain/subdomain, not the primary domain;
- set up SPF, DKIM, DMARC;
- start low volume;
- keep emails plain-text and specific;
- include a simple opt-out line;
- avoid deceptive subject lines;
- track replies manually at first.

Compliance notes:
- CAN-SPAM requires honest header/from information, non-deceptive subject lines, clear identification, a physical postal address, and a way to opt out.
- Gmail sender guidance expects authentication and respectful sending practices.

Useful references:
- FTC CAN-SPAM guide: https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/can-spam-act-compliance-guide-business
- Google sender guidelines: https://support.google.com/a/answer/81126
- Google sender FAQ: https://support.google.com/a/answer/14229414

### Channel B: LinkedIn

Purpose:
- warm up leads and reach founders when email is not visible.

Use for:
- founders of small agencies;
- strategists;
- creator/influencer leads;
- personal brand agencies.

Flow:

### Channel C: Website Contact Forms

### Channel D: Telegram / Communities

## Email Infrastructure Plan

### Minimum Setup

1. Dedicated sending domain:
   - example: `usewant2view.com`, `research.want2view.com`, or similar.

2. Mailbox:
   - `kirill@...` or `research@...`.

3. DNS:
   - SPF;
   - DKIM;
   - DMARC;
   - MX.

4. Tracking:
   - avoid heavy pixel/link tracking at the start;
   - replies matter more than opens.

5. Unsubscribe / opt-out:
   - simple line: `If this is not relevant, reply "not interested" and I will not follow up.`

6. Sending volume:
   - Week 1: 10-20/day;
   - Week 2: 20-40/day;
   - Week 3+: 50/day only if bounce/complaint risk is low.

### Do Not

- Do not send 500 emails from a fresh domain.
- Do not use the main product domain for risky cold outreach.
- Do not use fake personalization.
- Do not buy massive lists.
- Do not attach PDFs in first email.
- Do not lead with a calendar link.

## Demo Production Workflow

### When Lead Replies With Niche

1. Create internal task.
2. Run/prepare WANT2VIEW research.
3. Pull:
   - competitor channels;
   - outlier videos;
   - audience demand;
   - titles/hooks;
   - thumbnail/reference patterns;
   - 3 recommended angles.
4. Create 1-page mini brief.
5. Send brief.
6. Offer demo workspace.

### When Lead Does Not Reply But Is Score A

Use speculative demo only for 5-10 best leads.

Example:
> I guessed one niche from your site and ran a small scan. Here are three patterns that might be useful.

This is higher effort, so do it only for the best-fit accounts.

## Sales Conversation Handling

### If They Ask "What exactly do I get?"

Answer:
> A compact research brief for one niche: competitor/reference channels, fast-moving videos, repeated audience questions, title/hook/thumbnail patterns, and specific content/campaign angles your team can use.

### If They Ask "Is this just ChatGPT?"

Answer:
> ChatGPT starts from a prompt. WANT2VIEW starts from public video and channel signals: what has views, what competitors repeat, how topics are packaged, and what audience demand appears. AI helps structure the brief, but the input is market data.

### If They Ask Price

Answer:
> The easiest test is one full research report at $39. If it becomes useful for recurring client work, the active workspace is $99/month, and the agency package gives 5 workspaces.

### If They Say "We already do research"

Answer:
> That is exactly why I thought it might fit. I am not saying you lack research; I am testing whether WANT2VIEW can reduce the manual collection and make the first brief faster.
