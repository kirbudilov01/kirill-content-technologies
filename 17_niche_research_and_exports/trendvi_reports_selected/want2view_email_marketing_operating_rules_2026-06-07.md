# WANT2VIEW Email Marketing Operating Rules

Date: 2026-06-07

## Operating Principle

We are doing relevant B2B outreach, not mass spam.

Every message should be based on a real fit:
- their site says they do strategy/content/PR/influencer/YouTube work;
- we can name a plausible workflow bottleneck;
- we can offer a concrete free mini scan.

## Compliance Baseline

Use official guidance as the baseline:
- FTC CAN-SPAM guide: https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/can-spam-act-compliance-guide-business
- Google sender guidelines: https://support.google.com/a/answer/81126
- Google sender FAQ: https://support.google.com/a/answer/14229414

Practical rules:
- honest sender name;
- honest subject line;
- no fake relationship;
- include company/contact identity;
- include a simple opt-out;
- honor opt-outs;
- do not email people who ask not to be contacted;
- avoid scraped consumer/personal emails when business context is weak;
- keep a suppression list.

## Sending Setup

Recommended:
- separate outreach domain or subdomain;
- SPF/DKIM/DMARC configured;
- one mailbox per sender;
- low daily volume;
- manual review before first sends;
- no open tracking at the beginning;
- minimal links.

## Warm-Up Plan

Week 1:
- 10-15 emails/day.

Week 2:
- 20-30 emails/day if bounce risk is low.

Week 3:
- 30-50 emails/day only if replies and deliverability look healthy.

Do not blast.

## Message Style

Good:
- short;
- specific;
- plain text;
- one CTA;
- asks for a niche/channel/category;
- no deck;
- no attachment;
- no calendar link in first touch.

Bad:
- "I hope this email finds you well";
- generic AI/SaaS pitch;
- "revolutionize your content";
- 5 paragraphs of product features;
- fake flattery;
- "just checking in" follow-ups.

## Required Opt-Out Line

Simple:

> If this is not relevant, reply "not interested" and I will not follow up.

## Suppression List

Maintain a local suppression list:
- email;
- company;
- reason;
- date.

Never re-contact suppressed emails.
