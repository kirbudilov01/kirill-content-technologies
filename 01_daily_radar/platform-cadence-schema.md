# Content Distribution System: Platform Cadence

Цель: каждый день превращать один общий content radar в набор публикаций под разные платформы, без генерации огромного статичного backlog.

## Источники

1. AtlasRepo
   - свежие open-source проекты;
   - use cases;
   - proof для роликов и каруселей;
   - screencast-задачи для партнера.

2. want2view API
   - вирусные видео;
   - темы с ростом;
   - hook patterns;
   - конкуренты;
   - performance history.

3. News / live pulse
   - AI, agents, devtools, open-source, creator tools;
   - GitHub, OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Product Hunt, HN, Reddit.

4. Published history
   - что уже вышло;
   - что сработало;
   - какие темы не повторять слишком часто.

## Daily Flow

```text
AtlasRepo + want2view + news + прошлые публикации
        ↓
Daily Radar
        ↓
1 главная тема дня + 3-5 подтем
        ↓
Long YouTube topic
        ↓
Shorts/Reels/TikToks
        ↓
Instagram carousels
        ↓
X / Threads posts
        ↓
Partner screencast tasks
        ↓
Manual approval
        ↓
Postiz / platform publishing
        ↓
Analytics back to want2view / daily radar
```

## Weekly Cadence Per Account

### Instagram

Target:

- 5-6 carousels per week per account.
- 10-15 Reels per week per account.

Role:

- Carousels = save/share asset.
- Reels = reach/discovery.
- Stories = optional daily behind-the-scenes, polls, reposts.

Content types:

- "5 tools that replace paid SaaS"
- "AI is no longer a chat"
- "One-person company stack"
- "Repo radar: 3 tools worth saving"
- "Beginner explanation: what this trend means"

### TikTok

Target:

- 3 videos per day per account.
- 21 videos per week per account.

Role:

- Fast testing of hooks.
- More emotional / simple / mass-market framing.
- Less technical language than YouTube.

Daily structure:

1. Wow / future shock.
2. Useful tool or open-source replacement.
3. Opinion / hot take / trend reaction.

### YouTube Shorts

Target:

- Start: 3 Shorts per day per account.
- Scale: 5-6 Shorts per day per account after hooks/formats are validated.
- Start volume: 21 Shorts per week per account.
- Scale volume: 35-42 Shorts per week per account.

Role:

- Authority + discovery.
- Stronger AtlasRepo CTA than TikTok.
- More proof: GitHub, demo, screen recording.

Daily structure:

1. Repo/tool proof.
2. Trend explanation.
3. Open-source alternative / stack idea.

### YouTube Long-Form

Target:

- Start: 2-3 horizontal videos per day.
- Scale: publish as often as the recording/editing system can support, potentially hourly.
- Start volume: 14-21 long videos per week.
- Scale volume: constrained by quality, topic freshness, editing capacity, and platform feedback.

Role:

- Deep trust.
- Source asset for Shorts, posts, carousels.
- AtlasRepo positioning as discovery engine.

Good formats:

- "I found 10 open-source projects that show the future of work"
- "Open-source stack instead of paid SaaS"
- "AI is no longer a chat"
- "One person can now work like a small team"
- "GitHub is the future radar"

### X / Threads

Target:

- 4-5 posts per day on personal account.

Role:

- Thought leadership.
- Daily signal.
- Conversation and testing ideas before videos.

Daily structure:

1. Big thought.
2. Practical observation.
3. Open-source / AtlasRepo proof.
4. Contrarian take.
5. Optional question/poll.

Example post types:

- "AI will not stay a chat interface."
- "Open-source is becoming leverage for small teams."
- "The next skill is not prompting. It is assembling systems."
- "GitHub shows product futures before the market names them."

## Rollout Stages

### Stage 1: Start Mode

Use this when the system is still being stabilized.

Daily minimum:

- TikTok: 3 videos per day per account.
- YouTube Shorts: 3 Shorts per day per account.
- YouTube long-form: 2-3 videos per day.
- X / Threads: 4-5 personal posts per day.
- Instagram: distribute weekly target across the week, not necessarily daily.

Weekly target:

- Instagram: 5-6 carousels, 10-15 Reels per account.
- TikTok: 21 videos per account.
- YouTube Shorts: 21 Shorts per account.
- YouTube long-form: 14-21 videos total.
- X / Threads: 28-35 personal posts.

### Stage 2: Scale Mode

Use after several hooks and formats are validated.

Daily target:

- TikTok: 5-6 videos per day per account.
- YouTube Shorts: 5-6 Shorts per day per account.
- YouTube long-form: more than 3 videos per day if editing and topics support it.
- X / Threads: 6-10 posts per day.
- Instagram: more frequent Reels, carousels only if they remain high-quality.

### Stage 3: Aggressive Media Desk

Use only when the pipeline has enough source material, editing bandwidth, and quality control.

Target:

- Long-form can be produced very frequently, potentially hourly.
- Every long-form becomes multiple Shorts/Reels/TikToks.
- Every strong repo or topic becomes a carousel, X/Threads thread, and future follow-up.
- Analytics decide what gets repeated, not a fixed preplanned backlog.

Rule:

- Scale volume only when retention, saves, comments, or follower conversion justify it.
- Do not increase volume by making weaker content. Increase volume by repurposing stronger source material faster.

## Daily Production Package

Every day Codex should produce:

1. Main theme of the day.
2. 3 vertical video scripts.
3. 3 vertical video scene plans.
4. 4-5 X / Threads posts.
5. 1 carousel outline or full copy.
6. 1 long-form YouTube concept.
7. Partner screencast brief.
8. Distribution notes.

## Content Repurposing Logic

One long-form video should generate:

- 3-5 Shorts/Reels/TikToks;
- 1 carousel;
- 4-8 X/Threads posts;
- 1 forum/Reddit discussion prompt;
- 1 article/post-essay if the topic is strong enough.

One strong repo should generate:

- 1 short demo;
- 1 X/Threads post;
- 1 carousel slide;
- 1 segment inside long-form;
- 1 AtlasRepo database/use-case entry.

## Account Split

### Personal account

Voice:

- broader;
- more human;
- less technical;
- opinionated;
- future-of-work / creator / founder angle.

Best content:

- X / Threads;
- TikTok;
- Reels;
- personal YouTube intros.

### AtlasRepo account

Voice:

- more proof-driven;
- repo/tool oriented;
- clear open-source CTA.

Best content:

- YouTube Shorts;
- repo carousels;
- GitHub/tool demos;
- weekly radar posts.

### Partner / dev account

Voice:

- more technical;
- implementation-focused;
- setup, testing, limitations.

Best content:

- screencasts;
- deep repo walkthroughs;
- developer X posts;
- long-form technical support videos.

## Approval Rule

Generation can be automated.
Publishing should remain manual or semi-manual until:

- account permissions are clean;
- Postiz credentials are isolated;
- want2view feedback loop is stable;
- platform-specific quality checks are reliable.
