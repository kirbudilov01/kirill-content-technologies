# AtlasRepo Launch Funnel

Date: 2026-06-26

## Core Positioning

AtlasRepo should not be sold as a "repo catalog". That is the raw material.

The paid offer should be:

> AtlasRepo is a live AI/open-source opportunity engine that turns fresh repositories into ready-to-run business workflows, content systems, internal tools, and product ideas.

Short form:

> Stop bookmarking repos. Start launching systems.

The buyer does not want "more links". They want speed, clarity, and a shortcut from idea to usable system.

## Reference Model

The Skool reference works because it compresses the offer into five simple signals:

- A clear money/time promise.
- Named kits/systems instead of generic content.
- Daily support and fast answers.
- Visible urgency.
- Risk reversal through refunds or ROI framing.

For AtlasRepo, copy the structure, not the surface:

- Instead of "AI profit boardroom": `AtlasRepo Launch Lab` or `AtlasRepo Builder Lab`.
- Instead of "personal tech stack": `live repo intelligence + workflow packs + implementation support`.
- Instead of "I build automations for you": `we turn new repos into deployable workflow packs and help members adapt them`.

## The Offer

Primary offer:

> Join AtlasRepo Launch Lab and get weekly ready-to-run AI/open-source workflows built from the best new repos before they become obvious.

What members get:

- Live AtlasRepo database and category rankings.
- Weekly `Workflow Drops`: one practical system with repos, setup steps, prompts, templates, and business use cases.
- `Repo-to-Revenue Kits`: packaged workflows for agencies, founders, creators, consultants, and internal teams.
- Private Telegram/community access for questions and implementation.
- AI assistant that recommends a stack from the catalog for a user's task.
- Early access to FABRICBOT/payments/automation experiments when relevant.

Names for the first kits:

- `Content Engine Kit`: find trends, generate scripts, repurpose clips, schedule posts.
- `Lead Research Kit`: scrape/source prospects, enrich, score, and create outreach briefs.
- `AI Support Desk Kit`: self-hosted helpdesk + RAG + chat workflow.
- `MVP Builder Kit`: pick OSS blocks, deploy, wire auth/payments, ship a small product.
- `Agent Ops Kit`: monitor agent runs, logs, prompts, and handoffs.

## Entry Products

Use three layers, not one generic subscription:

1. Free lead magnet: `10 AI Workflows You Can Launch From Open Source This Week`.
2. Low-friction paid entry: `AtlasRepo Launch Lab Monthly`.
3. Scarce paid entry: `Lifetime Founding Access`, limited by batch count.

Pricing can stay close to current structure while testing:

- Monthly: `$39/mo` or local equivalent.
- Lifetime founder batch: `$99`, first 100 places.
- Later: raise lifetime to `$149`, `$249`, then close it.

The pricing page should make lifetime the obvious early-buyer option, but monthly should remain credible.

## Product Launch Formula Adaptation

Use a 7-day mini launch, not a long classical launch.

Pre-prelaunch:

- Publish public repo discoveries daily.
- Tease that the public catalog is only the first layer.
- Ask the audience what they would build if someone handed them ready repo stacks.

Prelaunch content:

1. `Opportunity`: "Open-source AI is moving faster than SaaS. Most people only save links. We turn them into systems."
2. `Transformation`: show one repo stack becoming a working workflow.
3. `Ownership`: "You do not need to code everything from zero. You need selection, setup, and a repeatable launch path."

Launch:

- Open founding access for 100 lifetime seats.
- Give a 72-hour founder bonus.
- Bonus should be concrete: `first 3 Workflow Drops`, `priority Telegram review`, `founder pricing locked`.

Post-launch:

- Publish weekly wins and workflow drops.
- Turn support questions into public content.
- Keep a waitlist or price-rise timer for the next batch.

## Funnel

Fast funnel:

1. Short video / X / Reddit / Telegram post.
2. Lead magnet page.
3. Email capture or Telegram bot capture.
4. Thank-you page with one immediate workflow preview.
5. 5-email sequence.
6. Pricing / checkout.
7. Member dashboard and Telegram/community invite.

Do not send cold traffic directly to "community access". Send it to a concrete workflow result first.

Recommended first landing path:

`/#/launch-lab`:

- Hero: `Launch useful AI systems from fresh open-source repos every week`.
- Above-fold proof: live repo count, update time, 3 example workflow kits.
- CTA: `Get the free workflow list`.
- Secondary CTA: `Join founding access`.
- Section: "What you get this week".
- Section: "How the lab works".
- Section: pricing.
- Section: FAQ and guarantee.

## Wow Effect

The first 60 seconds after signup should create a product feeling:

- User enters a goal: "I need a content automation system", "I need lead gen", "I need internal support bot".
- AtlasRepo returns 3-5 repos, a recommended stack, and a 3-step launch plan.
- Then paywall says: "Unlock the full workflow pack: setup guide, prompts, templates, and support."

This is better than a static PDF because the product itself demonstrates the value.

## Content Machine Without Founder

Daily system:

- Scout finds fresh repos.
- Hermes/social draft worker turns repo data into hooks.
- Content radar chooses 3 daily angles.
- AI generates scripts, threads, newsletter blurbs, and community posts.
- Human only approves, records optional face clips, or skips recording and uses screen/demo format.

Daily content types:

- `Repo of the day`: one tool, one use case, one CTA.
- `Build this instead of paying for X`: replace paid SaaS with OSS stack.
- `Open-source opportunity`: "this repo can become a micro-SaaS/agency offer/internal workflow".
- `Workflow teardown`: 3 repos combined into one business process.
- `Founder warning`: "do not install random repos blindly; use this checklist".

Each content piece should point to one CTA:

> Get the free AtlasRepo workflow list.

Not five CTAs.

## Email Sequence

Email 1: deliver lead magnet and show the core belief.

Subject: `Your 10 open-source AI workflows`

Point: the value is not links, it is choosing and launching.

Email 2: problem.

Subject: `Why most repo lists are useless`

Point: people collect tools, but do not ship systems.

Email 3: proof.

Subject: `Example: 3 repos into one content engine`

Point: show a concrete workflow.

Email 4: offer.

Subject: `Founding access is open`

Point: weekly workflow drops, private support, founder pricing.

Email 5: urgency.

Subject: `Founder batch closes soon`

Point: deadline, price rise, bonus expiration.

## Telegram Bot Flow

Bot entry:

1. "What do you want to build?"
2. Buttons: `Content`, `Lead gen`, `Support`, `Internal ops`, `MVP`, `AI agent`.
3. Bot returns a mini stack.
4. Bot asks for email.
5. Bot sends the lead magnet.
6. Bot offers founding access.

This can be built before full email automation and fits the user's Telegram-heavy workflow.

## Ads And Competitor Research

Use paid ads only after the organic funnel has a working lead magnet and checkout.

Research targets:

- Skool AI communities.
- AI automation agencies.
- No-code automation courses.
- Open-source/self-hosted communities.
- AI tool directories.

Tools:

- Google Ads Transparency Center for active creative patterns.
- Semrush or SpyFu for competitor search terms when budget allows.
- Manual scrape of public landing pages for headline/offer/price/guarantee patterns.

Ad angles to test:

- `Stop paying for 12 SaaS tools. Build from open-source blocks.`
- `Every week: one ready-to-run AI workflow from fresh GitHub repos.`
- `For founders who want AI systems, not another prompt pack.`
- `Turn GitHub repos into content engines, lead systems, and MVPs.`

## Success Metrics

Before ads:

- Lead page conversion: 20-40%.
- Assistant-to-email conversion: 25%+.
- Email-to-checkout click: 5-12%.
- Checkout-to-paid: 2-8% from warm leads.
- Daily content output: 3 short videos, 3-5 posts, 1 deeper thread or article.

First real goal:

> 100 founding members or enough paid buyers to prove that "workflow drops from live repo intelligence" is a sellable promise.

## Key Decision

The main marketing move is to stop selling the database as the product.

Sell the transformation:

> "Every week we turn fresh open-source AI repos into deployable systems you can use for content, lead generation, support, internal ops, and MVP launches."
