# AtlasRepo / FABRICBOT Global Marketing OS

Date: 2026-06-26

Purpose: build a full marketing operating system around AtlasRepo/FABRICBOT before changing the landing page. This document is meant to be fed into future agents for SEO, video production, social publishing, avatars, analytics, and funnel automation.

## 1. Strategic Core

The product should be marketed as a system, not as a directory.

Core promise:

> Every week, AtlasRepo turns fresh open-source AI repositories into practical workflows, content systems, lead systems, internal tools, and MVP launch kits.

Short slogan:

> Stop bookmarking repos. Start launching systems.

What we are really selling:

- Speed: skip weeks of research.
- Clarity: know which repos are alive and useful.
- Leverage: combine repos into working systems.
- Ownership: replace fragile SaaS subscriptions with self-hosted or adaptable tools.
- Distribution: turn every discovery into videos, posts, SEO pages, and offers.

Main market:

- Founders building with AI.
- Indie hackers and vibe coders.
- AI automation agencies.
- Creators who need content/distribution systems.
- Developers who want useful open-source stacks.
- Small teams replacing expensive SaaS tools.

## 2. Offer Architecture

Use a ladder, not one product.

Free:

- Public AtlasRepo scanner/catalog.
- Free lead magnet: `10 AI/Open-Source Workflows You Can Launch This Week`.
- Free daily/weekly repo discovery content.

Entry paid:

- `AtlasRepo Launch Lab Monthly`.
- Weekly workflow drops.
- Private community/chat.
- Setup guides, prompts, templates, demo paths.

Founder paid:

- `Lifetime Founding Access`.
- Limited batch: first 100 seats.
- Founder price locked.
- Priority workflow requests.

Higher-ticket later:

- `Done-with-you Workflow Sprint`.
- `Agency Stack Setup`.
- `Internal Automation Audit`.
- `Custom Repo-to-Workflow Pack`.

## 3. The Marketing Machine

Everything should run through one repeatable pipeline:

1. Signal capture:
   - AtlasRepo scout data.
   - GitHub trending/recent repos.
   - Hacker News, Reddit, Product Hunt, YouTube, Google Trends-style signals.
   - want2view performance data when available.

2. Topic scoring:
   - Is it hot now?
   - Does it fit AtlasRepo?
   - Can it be demoed visually?
   - Can it become a business workflow?
   - Can it support both a Short and a long video?
   - Can it become an SEO page?

3. Content packaging:
   - Long-form YouTube episode.
   - 3-7 Shorts/Reels/TikToks.
   - X/Threads posts.
   - Telegram post.
   - SEO article or repo/use-case page.
   - Email/newsletter.
   - Community prompt.

4. Funnel routing:
   - Every piece points to one CTA.
   - Default CTA: free AtlasRepo workflow scanner/list.
   - Warmer CTA: Launch Lab founding access.

5. Measurement:
   - Views and retention.
   - Clicks to scanner/lead magnet.
   - Email/Telegram captures.
   - Checkout starts.
   - Paid conversions.
   - Topics that create comments/questions.

## 4. Content Pillars

Pillar 1: Open-source replacements.

Format:

> Stop paying for X. Use this open-source stack instead.

Examples:

- Notion replacement.
- Zapier/n8n automation stack.
- Intercom/support bot stack.
- Social scheduler stack.
- AI coding/workspace stack.

Pillar 2: Repo-to-business.

Format:

> This GitHub repo can become a micro-SaaS, agency offer, or internal workflow.

Examples:

- Lead enrichment system.
- Content repurposing workflow.
- AI research/report generator.
- Self-hosted analytics.
- Internal assistant.

Pillar 3: Weekly workflow drops.

Format:

> This week's AtlasRepo workflow: 3 repos, 1 practical system.

Examples:

- Content Engine Kit.
- Lead Research Kit.
- AI Support Desk Kit.
- MVP Builder Kit.
- Agent Ops Kit.

Pillar 4: AI agent/product radar.

Format:

> What changed this week in AI/open-source tools, and what it means for builders.

Pillar 5: Build in public.

Format:

> We found, tested, packaged, and shipped this workflow.

This creates trust because people see the system working.

## 5. Long-Form YouTube System

Goal: make YouTube the authority layer.

Cadence:

- Minimum: 2 long videos per week.
- Aggressive: 5-7 long videos per week if avatar/screencast automation works.

Default structures:

1. `I found 7 open-source AI tools that can replace a small team`
2. `Build a content engine from GitHub repos`
3. `Stop paying for these SaaS tools: open-source alternatives tested`
4. `I used AtlasRepo to find AI agent stacks for founders`
5. `This repo can become a $5k automation agency offer`

Each long video should produce:

- 5 Shorts.
- 1 X thread.
- 3 short posts.
- 1 SEO article.
- 1 email.
- 1 community discussion.

Long video script pattern:

- 0:00-0:30: broad problem and stakes.
- 0:30-1:30: promise and map.
- 1:30-8:00: repo/tool demos.
- 8:00-12:00: combine into workflow.
- 12:00-14:00: caveats, setup difficulty, who should use it.
- 14:00-end: AtlasRepo method and CTA.

Native AtlasRepo mention:

- Early: "I found these while scanning repos in AtlasRepo."
- Later: "The point is not one repo. The point is the repeatable workflow discovery engine."
- CTA: "The free AtlasRepo scanner/list is linked in the channel header."

## 6. Short-Form System

Goal: make Shorts/Reels/TikToks the discovery layer.

Cadence:

- Minimum: 3 vertical videos per day.
- Aggressive: 5-10 per day once avatar pipeline is reliable.

Shorts patterns:

- `Did you know you can stop paying for [tool]?`
- `I found an open-source repo that does [specific job].`
- `This GitHub repo is basically [business workflow] in a box.`
- `Most people save AI tools. This is how you turn one into a system.`
- `One repo, one use case, one business idea.`

Every Short needs:

- First 2 seconds: normal-person problem.
- One repo/tool.
- One visible proof.
- One caveat or grounded line.
- One comment prompt.
- One AtlasRepo CTA.

Avoid:

- Generic AI hype.
- Too many repos in one Short.
- Repeated endings.
- Same hook template every day.

## 7. SEO System

Goal: make Google/AI search capture intent that social content creates.

SEO clusters:

- `open-source alternatives to [SaaS]`
- `best open-source AI agents for [use case]`
- `self-hosted [category] tools`
- `GitHub repos for [workflow]`
- `AI automation workflow for [business problem]`
- `how to build [system] with open-source tools`

Page types:

- Repo profile page.
- Category page.
- Use-case page.
- Comparison page.
- Workflow pack page.
- Weekly radar article.

Each SEO page should include:

- Search intent answer in first screen.
- Recommended stack.
- Pros/cons.
- Setup difficulty.
- Alternatives.
- Update date.
- AtlasRepo CTA.

Rule:

> SEO content must be useful even if the reader never pays.

That creates trust and makes the paid layer feel like acceleration, not a lock.

## 8. Social Account Roles

Do not make every account post the same thing.

YouTube:

- Authority and discovery.
- Long videos + Shorts.

X/Twitter:

- Builder-native insights, threads, hot takes, repo drops.

Threads:

- Simpler creator/founder versions of X posts.

Telegram:

- Core community, launches, daily drops, direct trust.

Reddit:

- Discussion, not hard selling.
- Post useful repo/use-case breakdowns where relevant.

LinkedIn:

- Business angle: cost savings, internal tools, automation, case studies.

Newsletter:

- Weekly digest: repo radar + workflow drop + founder note + CTA.

## 9. Digital Avatar / Local Production

Use avatars for volume, but do not let them destroy trust.

Avatar use cases:

- Shorts from scripted repo discoveries.
- Screencast narration.
- Multilingual versions.
- Daily recap videos.

Human/founder use cases:

- Big launch videos.
- Trust-building stories.
- Product announcements.
- High-ticket offer videos.

Production pipeline:

1. Topic selected from AtlasRepo/want2view/news.
2. Script generated.
3. Screencast brief generated.
4. Local avatar/narration generated.
5. Captions and cuts generated.
6. Human approves top candidates.
7. Scheduler posts.
8. Analytics feeds back into topic scoring.

Important:

- Do not create fake claims of personal experience.
- Disclose AI/avatar use when platform rules or trust require it.
- Keep demos real and source-grounded.

## 11. Agent Roles To Build

Radar Agent:

- Pulls fresh repos, news, YouTube/want2view signals.
- Scores topics.
- Outputs daily content slate.

Script Agent:

- Writes long-form scripts, Shorts, X posts, Telegram posts.
- Preserves AtlasRepo voice.

SEO Agent:

- Builds keyword clusters.
- Creates page briefs and article drafts.
- Maps each video to search pages.

Screencast Agent:

- Produces recording briefs.
- Lists exact screens, repo pages, demo paths, warnings.

Avatar Production Agent:

- Turns approved scripts into avatar/narration jobs.
- Generates multilingual versions.

Publisher Agent:

- Schedules posts.
- Adds UTMs.
- Tracks asset IDs and platform URLs.

Analytics Agent:

- Reads performance.
- Finds winning hooks, topics, and CTAs.
- Feeds the next daily radar.

Funnel Agent:

- Manages emails, Telegram bot messages, offers, urgency windows.

```json
  "seo_page": "open-source-content-engine",

```

This keeps all agents aligned.
