# AtlasRepo / FABRICBOT Full Marketing Strategy Map

Date: 2026-06-26

Purpose: one big marketing map for AtlasRepo/FABRICBOT: product narrative, promo mechanics, content engine, social channels, website architecture, funnel, SEO, community, launches, agents, and analytics.

## 0. One-Screen System

```text
SCOUT CORE
  ↓
Repo + trend + audience signal database
  ↓
Workflow Intelligence Layer
  ↓
Content + SEO + Guides + Offers + Product Roadmap
  ↓
Social distribution + YouTube + Search + Community
  ↓
Free scanner / lead magnet / Telegram capture
  ↓
$39 monthly → $99 builder sprint → $299 annual OS
  ↓
Member workflow packs + Codex/Claude playbooks + agents
  ↓
Usage + analytics + questions + conversions
  ↓
Back into Scout scoring and next promo cycle
```

The core idea:

> Scout finds the market. Marketing explains the opportunity. Paid product helps users implement it.

## 1. Strategic Position

Do not position AtlasRepo as a directory.

Position it as:

> AI Workflow Intelligence OS for builders.

Long version:

> AtlasRepo scans the open-source AI market, finds useful repositories, turns them into category-specific workflows, and helps builders implement them with Codex, Claude, and automation agents.

Public marketing promise:

> Stop collecting AI tools. Start launching workflows.

Internal product promise:

> Every repo discovery can become a workflow, guide, video, SEO page, social post, and paid offer.

## 5. Content Engine

The weekly workflow pack is the source asset.

```text
1 workflow pack
  → 1 long YouTube video
  → 5-10 Shorts
  → 1 public guide
  → 1 internal guide
  → 1 email/newsletter
  → 1 X thread
  → 3-5 X/Threads posts
  → 1 Telegram drop
  → 1 LinkedIn post
  → 1 Reddit discussion angle
  → 1 SEO page
```

Minimum weekly cadence:

- 1 workflow pack;
- 1 public guide;
- 2 long video ideas or 1 finished long video;
- 10 Shorts;
- 15 social posts;
- 1 newsletter;
- 1 Telegram sequence.

Aggressive cadence later:

- 2 workflow packs/week;
- 3-5 long videos/week;
- 5-10 Shorts/day;
- daily Telegram and X/Threads;
- 2-3 SEO pages/week.

## 6. Content Pillars

### Pillar 1: Open-Source Replacements

Hook:

> Stop paying for X. Use this open-source workflow instead.

Examples:

- Notion replacement stack.
- Zapier/n8n replacement stack.
- Buffer/Postiz content stack.
- Intercom/support bot stack.
- Retool/Appsmith internal tool stack.

Hook:

Examples:

- content repurposing system;
- self-hosted analytics;
- AI support desk;
- report generator.

### Pillar 3: Codex/Claude Implementation

Hook:

> Finding the repo is step one. Here is how Codex/Claude can help you implement it.

Examples:

- turn README into setup tasks;
- generate Docker/deploy checklist;
- adapt repo for a business use case;
- create API wrapper;
- build interface around OSS.

### Pillar 4: Weekly Radar

Hook:

> The open-source AI market moved again. Here is what builders should notice.

Examples:

- top 5 AI repos this week;
- new agent frameworks;
- self-hosted tools;
- local AI tools;
- devtools.

### Pillar 5: Build In Public

Hook:

> We found a repo, tested it, packaged it, and turned it into a workflow.

Examples:

- 7-day content engine build;
- lead gen workflow build;
- MVP builder stack;
- support bot setup.

## 12. Analytics Loop

- best topic;
- best hook;
- best platform;
- best CTA;
- best objection;
- most requested workflow;
- highest-converting source;
- next workflow pack;
- next launch angle.

- what workflow pack to build;
- what SEO page to write;
- what offer to promote;
- what video to record.

## 15. Main Risks

Risk 1:

- becoming a generic AI tool directory.

Fix:

- always translate repo into workflow and implementation.

Risk 2:

- producing lots of content with no offer path.

Fix:

- every content asset maps to a CTA.

Risk 3:

- too much automation before message-market fit.

Fix:

- first 30 days are controlled and analytics-driven.

Risk 4:

- overpromising implementation.

Fix:

- state setup difficulty, risks, and limits honestly.

Risk 5:

- social accounts all posting duplicated content.

Fix:

- each channel has a specific role.

## 16. North Star

The whole system should make this sentence true:

> AtlasRepo is the place where builders discover the best open-source AI opportunities, understand what they can become, and get the workflow playbooks to launch them with Codex, Claude, and agents.
